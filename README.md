# Inventory-Management-Dashboard-Excel
📌 Project Overview
This Excel dashboard tracks inventory data of 46 products across multiple categories — including gaming peripherals, computer hardware, and office accessories. The goal was to monitor stock availability, identify slow-moving inventory, flag low-stock alerts, and track units sold vs opening stock for better supply chain decisions.

📊 Key Metrics Tracked
Metric                    Value
Total Products             46
Total Opening Stock        1,655 units
Total Units Sold           314
Total Stock Available      2,004 units
Total Inventory Cost      ₹3,59,760

🔍 Key Insights from the Dashboard

Highest Opening Stock: Smartphone and Printer Cable have the highest opening stock — potential overstock risk if sales don't match
Stock Availability: Nearly 50-50 split between available and unavailable stock — significant portion of inventory is out of stock, risking lost sales
Top Selling Products: Units sold chart shows some products like Gaming Keyboard selling significantly more than others — demand-supply mismatch visible
Slow-Moving Inventory: Several products in the ₹45,000+ range show very low sales velocity — flagged for clearance or reorder pause
Purchase Stock Distribution: Pie chart shows purchase stock spread across reorder quantities of 6 to 30 units — smaller batches dominate
Units Sold vs Opening Stock: Line chart reveals products with high opening stock but low sales — classic dead stock indicator


🛠️ Tools & Techniques Used

Tool: Microsoft Excel
Visuals Used: Bar chart, pie chart, line chart, horizontal bar chart
Techniques: Pivot Tables, dynamic Slicers for product-wise filtering
Data Cleaning: Standardized product names, calculated available stock using formulas


💼 Business Use Case
This dashboard is designed for inventory managers and supply chain teams to:

Monitor real-time stock availability across 46 product SKUs
Identify dead/slow-moving stock worth ₹45,000+ for clearance
Plan reorders based on units sold vs current stock levels
Track purchase patterns to optimize supplier ordering quantities
